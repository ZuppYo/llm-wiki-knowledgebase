# Philips AUT8990 Dual Filter Paths

Compiled from [Philips AUT8990 Dual-Mode Water Filter 101](../Topics/philips-aut8990-dual-mode-water-filter-101.md).

## Problem the design solves

Using **RO-only** water for rinsing vegetables, dishes, or high-volume sink tasks wastes expensive RO cartridge life. AUT8990 routes **light filtration (MF)** for kitchen use and **full RO** only when drinking.

Source emphasizes rural / non-municipal water users and pesticide residue on produce — MF path for rinsing, RO for drinking.

## Two cartridges, multi-stage each

Philips markets **two cartridges**, not two single-purpose filters:

| Cartridge | Label | Stages (source) | Output path |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| **4-in-1** | Composite MF | Coarse sediment → PP fine → carbon (odor/chemical/color) → post-carbon (pH) | **MF only** — bypasses RO |
| **AQP** | Aquaporin RO | Five RO stages + **built-in remineralization** in one stick | **RO** — water passes **4-in-1 first**, then AQP |

**QuickTwist** bayonet: open cover, push up, twist to remove; reverse to install. No tools.

## Faucet selector

Dual-hose faucet; lever position (source):

| Position | Mode | Filter path |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Back | **MF** | 4-in-1 only |
| Center | Off | — |
| Forward | **RO** | 4-in-1 → AQP RO |

Tubing colors in demo: green = MF, blue = RO (after both stages), white = inlet, gray = drain.

## Flow behavior

| Mode | Pressure / flow (source) |
|------|--------------------------|
| **MF** | Near household tap pressure (no booster) |
| **RO** | Internal motor boosts pressure; **~2 L/min** RO output |

## Cost-saving rationale (source)

- MF rinsing avoids running full RO for every sink task → **extends AQP cartridge life**.
- Integrated remineralization in AQP cartridge avoids separate **mineral heads** (~1,000+ THB every ~3 months on older Philips RO setups cited in review).

## Contaminant claims (AQP path)

Marketing in source: **110+ contaminant types** — chlorine, bacteria, viruses, pesticides, scale, fluoride, long-lived chemicals.
