# Rubberwood Spray Painting Workflow

Compiled from [Rubberwood Spray Painting 101](../Topics/rubberwood-spray-painting-101.md). Material: **rubberwood plywood** — core surface may start uneven, but the demonstrated system targets a smooth industrial-style finish.

## Phase 1 — Putty and surface prep

1. **Hand-check** the panel: no open holes; gaps must be filled completely with putty (สีโป๊).
2. **Mix putty** with hardener in moderation (demo mentions a fast hardener such as “3A”); too much hardener dries before you can work.
3. **Fill and scrape** with a putty knife; let harden. If it shrinks, come back and repair.
4. **Cover the full face** with putty, then level until smooth. Let dry.
5. **Sand** with **180 grit** (hand sheet or machine; demo also shows coarser paper on a power sander for bulk removal).
6. **Stop when only wood shows** — no visible putty left on the surface. Over-sanding exposes defects later; under-filled spots also show after paint.
7. **Curves and profiles:** finish by hand, following the shape, where the machine cannot reach.

Typical putty rounds before primer: **1–2** passes, each followed by sanding to 180.

## Phase 2 — Sealer and primer

1. **Mix sealer** (ซิลเลอร์ — thick cream consistency in the demo). Stir thoroughly (back of the mix first, then front).
2. **Spray technique:** edges and corners first, then rotate through all four faces. Spray **the bottom first** on each pass when possible.
3. **Primer choice:** for **light or white top coats**, use **white primer** (รองพื้นขาว; demo uses a W-10 type). Dark opaque top coats use a different primer path (not fully shown).
4. **Thinning ratio (spray system):** about **1 part paint : 1.5 parts thinner** — no scale needed unlike two-part polyurethane systems.
5. **First coat is light:** let it soak for adhesion; build coverage in **rounds 2–3**. Do not try to hide everything in one wet pass.
6. **Stir primer** before spraying to improve adhesion and evenness.

Primer reveals remaining pores, unfilled spots, or places sanded too aggressively — expect repair loops.

## Phase 3 — Repair loop after primer

1. After a primer coat dries, **defects “show through”** (ฟ้อง). Fill again with putty where needed.
2. **Sand smooth** (hand work on small defects; wet sand mentioned between some rounds).
3. **Second putty product** in the demo (e.g. “10 Pro” grade) for touch-up after primer.
4. **Re-primer:** for multi-face furniture the instructor cites roughly **1–2 primer rounds + up to 3 finish rounds** on large four-sided work; simpler statuary-style pieces may finish in **2 primer + 2 top** passes.

## Phase 4 — Top coat (matte white demo)

1. After primer is acceptable, spray **final color** — demo finishes in **matte white** (สีขาวด้าน).
2. **White and light colors** often need **~3 top coats** if coverage is weak; a good primer base reduces the number needed.
3. If mixed paint begins to skin/harden in the cup, add a small amount of **thinner or prior mix** to keep it workable.
4. **Dry between coats**; demo stresses patience — matte wet look lightens as it dries.

## Phase 5 — Inspection and optional finish

1. Inspect after **two top coats**; minor defects may still be acceptable for casual household work.
2. **Optional:** matte **lacquer** (แลคเกอร์ด้าน) for extra protection — demo piece could stop before this step.
3. **Touch-up sand:** light pass with **320 grit** (400 optional) to clear fine defects; higher-end gloss work demands more sanding discipline than quick household jobs.

## Quick reference

| Stage | Grit / product | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| After putty | 180 | Remove all visible putty |
| Primer | White primer for light tops | Light first coat, build up |
| Repair | Putty + re-primer | Expected, not a failure |
| Top coat | User color (white demo) | 2–3 passes typical for white |
| Touch-up | 320–400 | Optional; job-quality dependent |

## Spray habits called out in source

- Rotate the piece; hit **edges before flat faces**.
- **Bottom surfaces first** when spraying.
- Keep coats thin early; **adhesion before opacity**.
- Rubberwood plywood is presented as a **budget-friendly** substrate suitable for nationwide/household production work when the full sequence is followed.
